1. Group By

What’s up with GROUP BY? Is it as straightforward as it sounds or is there some dark layer hiding underneath that will haunt your SQL statement and return you ‘brie’ when you were expecting ‘Gouda’. SQL acts like its as straight forward as vanilla but we all know there’s some chunky monkey hiding beneath the surface.

Basics:

Lots of times we have more than one record for a certain attribute. For example, there could be 25 players on the Yankees in a league with hundreds of players and I just want to know the best on base percentage for the yankees.

OR what if I’m trying to see how many current members are in Earth Wind & Fire from a database that has that everyone who has ever played in the group.

GROUP BY is the key to finding out this kind of information

2. HAVING So what’s the deal with using HAVING. I know it replaces WHERE sometimes but what’s the rule… when should I be using it?

  1. Where does not work with aggregates… For example: SELECT animal_type, sum(Qty) from Animals_in_zoo GROUP BY animal_type WHERE sum(qty) > 10

This does not work. You need a HAVING instead of WHERE SELECT animal_type, sum(Qty) from Animals_in_zoo GROUP BY animal_type HAVING sum(qty) > 10

  1. One of the SELECT items has an AS after it.

SELECT animal_type, animal AS animalize from Animals_in_zoo GROUP BY animal_type HAVING animalize = ‘Duck’
